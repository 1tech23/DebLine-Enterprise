Wired Air-Data Computer System

WADCS

-------------------------------------------------------

WADCS is, essentially, the modem that connects your device to the computer center, the place where the developer upgrades and updates DebLine OS, and where all of the data is stored. If your device has wireless connections, for both WiFi and Bluetooth, you can connect to the modem in order to transfer data. If it does not, you can use a USB cable, or an Ethernet cable, or even a Dial-Up phone line wire.

However, WADCS is much faster, and more effecient than, say, the average fifth generation (5G) broadband cellular networks

Instead of using a wireless broadband network attached to a few pieces of metal, WADCS uitlizes quantum technology. A supercomputer, a quantum computer if you will, that can calculate both zeroes and ones simultaneously is the central core for all of the operations. This quantum computer is called Q2. Every Q2 unit has an additional sequence at the end of it's name. For example, the first sucessful Q2 unit was Q2-1A. Each Q2 unit has a database of nearly ten exabytes (10000 petabytes, 10000000 terrabytes). This quantum computer is connected to a transmittal station, to transmit and recieve the broadband data. This data is sent to the MSC, which send it to a modem. The modem, located at your nearest telephone pole, recieves the broadband, and directs it to your WADCS center, which should be wired to an Ethernet port, in your wall.

Main-System Central

MSC

----------------------------------

MSC is the center of computations. It is the big computer. The one that processes all of the data sent to and recieved from all devices on the WADCS network. It is also a quantum computer, yet, it is not nearly as advanced as the Q2, the quantum computer centered in space to keep it cooled. This quantum computer is called Q3. Each Q3 unit has a sequence at the end. The first Q3 unit was called Q3-1A. Like Q2-1A, it was centered in space.
